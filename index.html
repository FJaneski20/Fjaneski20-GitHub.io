Unit 1 & 2 Vocabulary

Bit & Byte: The smallest units of digital data storage. 8 bits = 1 byte.

Roundoff: Small errors that occur when representing numbers digitally.

Analog Data: Continuous data like sound waves, compared to digital data.

Lossless vs. Lossy: Types of data compression; lossless keeps all data, lossy removes some.

Metadata: Data about data, like image size or time created.

Computing Device & Computer Network: Devices and systems that connect and share information.

Path, Protocol, Fault Tolerant, Scalability: Terms describing how networks handle data, errors, and growth.

Bandwidth, URL, Routers, Packets, IP: All about how information moves through the internet.

Physical Network, HTTP, WWW, DNS, TCP, UDP: Infrastructure and rules that allow the internet to function.
